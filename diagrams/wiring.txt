```
### Installation Tips
1. **Polarity matters!** 
   - **Negative (-)** leg → GND
   - **Positive (+)** leg → VCC (3.3V)
   - The negative leg is usually shorter and marked with a stripe on the capacitor body

2. **Keep leads short** - solder the capacitor as close to the nRF24L01+ module as possible for maximum effectiveness

3. **Double-check voltage rating** - ensure your 100μF capacitor is rated for at least 6.3V (10V or 16V is common and fine)
```

```
## Additional Power Considerations

Since you have 100μF capacitors, here are some extra tips:

### 1. **Multiple Capacitors** (Optional but recommended)
If you have stability issues, you can use multiple capacitors in parallel:
- 100μF electrolytic (bulk capacitance - handles large current spikes)
- 0.1μF ceramic (if available - handles high-frequency noise)
```
VCC ──┬── 100μF ──┬── GND
      │           │
      └── 0.1μF ──┘
```

### 2. **External Power for nRF24L01+ with PA+LNA**
If you're using the nRF24L01+ with external antenna (PA+LNA version), it draws more current. Consider:

**Option A: Power from Arduino 3.3V** (your current setup)
- Good for basic/standard nRF24L01+ modules
- May struggle with PA+LNA modules under heavy load
- Your 100μF cap helps significantly

**Option B: Separate 3.3V regulator** (if you have power issues)
```
Arduino 5V ──→ AMS1117-3.3 ──→ nRF24L01+ VCC
                    |
                   GND ──────→ Common GND
                    |
                  100μF
